How To Build A Computer Step by step instructions for everyone...

It's easier than you think...

I think the number one question I get asked when someone is thinking about building their own computer is..."Is it hard?" I really like that I get to say "No, it's mostly all plug in and power on!!" Once you understand what the different components are for and have a slight idea of how they work your golden!! There is almost nothing complicated about building a computer. I have even helped my mother build one (and that's saying a lot...). This website is meant to be a one stop website for instructions on building your first pc, as well as some of my favorite pc build sheets that you can use to build an epic pc, at a fraction of the custom pc build shop price.

Planning your computer build...

Building a computer does however take a little work, most of which is planning. The first thing I ask my customer is "What's your budget?". Please understand that while you will save a HUGE amount of money by building your own computer (especially a gaming computer), you are still going to spend some money if you want a competitive gaming computer. An awesome gaming computer that will be competitive for a few years and future proof for many many years can be built for $1,250-$2,500. While gaming computers are a little spendy, you can still build one hell of a computer in the $750-$1,000 range that will still be consider "future proof" for quite a few years. If alls you do is play games on Facebook, watch Youtube videos and cruise the information highway, then a budget of $350-$500 is more than enough to keep you happy for many years. Once you have decided what your computer build budget will be you can pick one of the featured builds below, or, read them all and mix and match the parts for the perfect computer for you!! I have selected 8-10 builds for Intel CPU lovers and 8-10 builds for AMD lovers. If you don't have a favourite I would suggest going with the AMD builds. They are cheaper and you definitely get more bang for your buck, especially when it comes to CPU's and GPU's (graphic cards).

Parts Rundown

For the complete build sheets, check the next section below titled "desktop builds", or you can just select the build you want to see from the menu above. Just for a super quick reference these are the parts you will need to have to build your own computer:

  • Case
  • Motherboard
  • CPU (Processor)
  • CPU Cooler
  • Ram
  • HDD/SSD or both
  • Power Supply
  • GPU (graphics card)
  • DVD/CD Drive

After you have collected, amassed, hoarded and bartered to get your computer build components, it is time to make sure you have an adequate workspace free of debris and junk to work in. The kitchen table is usually best, but a counter will do just fine in most cases if there is enough light. The only tools you will need will be a small screw driver set, you may even be able to get away with just one small Phillips screw driver.

Assembling Your New Computer

The most awesome things about assembling a computer is that much of it really is plug-and-play, and very few things can go wrong, and then usually it's only a minor setback, like re-routing a cord so you can reach it's intended plug. The first thing we do is open our computer case. Each case has a manual that comes with it, and I have never encountered one that took more then 2 minutes to glance at to figure out how to take the case apart. Most of them include very detailed exploded views. By looking through the manual, you will quickly get an idea of where everything should go. A lot of the builds I feature here use the NZXT full tower case. This case is remarkably easy to disassemble. You simple pull the front "face" off from the bottom to reveal the dvd drives, Then if you look on the "back" where all the plugs are, you will see two thumb screws for each right and left side panel. Remove the two thumb screws from one side, and put them somewhere safe. You are now able to slide the panel back about an inch, which will release it to fall away from the case. Repeat this process to remove the other side panel. The top panel pulls straight off just like the front face, except you have to pull up, not forward.

Installing the Motherboard

Installing the motherboard into the case is the first real part of the computer installation process. This is the piece that all your other computer pieces will be connecting to, so it has to go in first. When you take out your motherboard, you will immediately notice that it is very complex, and it has a lot of holes in it. This next step is very important!!

Read The Manual!

Before you actually do anything besides admire your motherboard, you need to read the manual!! The manual will tell you which holes to use for your case. Usually I gently set the motherboard down on the case where it will go and I can tell which holes in the case will need the standoffs.

Install The Standoffs

These are small "risers" That keep your motherboard separate from your case, to prevent wear, heating and shorting issues. This is a necessary step so don't skip it!! Install the standoffs into the case, under the cosponsoring motherboard holes but do not put them any any other case hole, if you have extra just save them in your motherboard box(That's where I store all my extra connectors). Screw them in as tight as you can get them with your fingers.

Install The Motherboard

After a standoff is installed for each motherboard hole, it is time to put the motherboard on the standoffs very gently and double check all the motherboard holes have a standoff under them. Double check with your case and motherboard manual to make sure you have the correct oriantation. You can now use the small screw driver and the screws provided to screw down your mother board to the standoffs!

Installing the motherboard is relatively simple. You find the part of the case that the motherboard will be attached to, and use one of the screwdrivers in your toolkit to screw it in. In case you don't know where the motherboard will be attached, or it isn't immediately obvious, your manual will help you through this step.
